# ProfitNet Email and Newsletter Onboarding Sequence

Status date: 2026-06-27

This sequence introduces new community members to ProfitNet through education, verification, and careful participation.

Recommended cadence:

- Email 1: immediately after signup
- Email 2: day 2
- Email 3: day 4
- Email 4: day 7
- Email 5: day 10
- Email 6: day 14
- Email 7: day 21

## Email 1: Welcome To ProfitNet

Subject: Welcome to ProfitNet
Preview: Start with the public record, not the hype.

```markdown
Hi,

Welcome to ProfitNet.

ProfitNet is built around a simple rule: public claims should be easy to verify.

The PNET dossier collects asset facts, source links, tokenomics claims, market snapshots, public claims policy, and open verification gaps.

Start here:
[link to dossier README]

Best first step:
Read the verification handbook and look for the status labels:

- Verified
- Snapshot only
- Needs verification
- Needs on-chain verification
- Needs methodology review

Educational content only. No investment advice, no deposit request, no passive-income claim, no guaranteed value, and no exchange-listing claim.
```

## Email 2: The 10-Minute ASA Review

Subject: A 10-minute Algorand ASA review routine
Preview: ASA ID, supply, controls, holders, and market snapshots.

```markdown
Hi,

Here is a simple way to review any Algorand ASA:

1. Confirm the ASA ID.
2. Check name, unit, decimals, and total supply.
3. Review manager, reserve, freeze, and clawback fields.
4. Inspect holder concentration.
5. Review market pages as dated snapshots.
6. Mark unresolved claims as Needs verification.

This routine is useful because someone else can repeat it.

Read the full guide:
[link to article]

Reminder:
Verification reduces confusion. It does not remove market, liquidity, project, or execution risk.
```

## Email 3: Tokenomics Without Shortcuts

Subject: Tokenomics is not one number
Preview: Separate total supply, burns, locks, liquidity, and methodology.

```markdown
Hi,

A tokenomics review gets stronger when categories stay separate.

Total supply is not circulating supply.
Burned supply is not the same as inaccessible-account supply.
Locked supply needs lock evidence.
Liquidity needs pool evidence.
Circulating supply needs methodology.

The PNET dossier marks unresolved tokenomics claims instead of converting them into facts too early.

Read the tokenomics section:
[link to tokenomics doc]

Useful phrase:
Observed on [date]. Source: [link]. Status: Needs verification.
```

## Email 4: Market Snapshots

Subject: Market data should have a date
Preview: Why liquidity, volume, and TVL should be treated as snapshots.

```markdown
Hi,

Market data changes quickly.

Liquidity, TVL, volume, price, and routes can be useful at capture time and misleading later.

ProfitNet uses snapshot language:

Source:
Capture date:
Observed value:
Method:
Status: Snapshot only

This makes old data useful without pretending it is current forever.

Read the market snapshot guide:
[link to market snapshots]
```

## Email 5: Contribution Credits, Safely Explained

Subject: What contribution credits are, and what they are not
Preview: Access and reputation records, not deposits or yield.

```markdown
Hi,

The planned PNET Community Contribution Protocol is framed as a non-custodial Algorand application for app-local contribution credits.

That means:

- credits may support access and reputation workflows,
- credits are not PNET tokens,
- credits are not deposits,
- credits are not yield,
- credits are not passive income,
- credits are not guaranteed value,
- credits are not claims on treasury assets.

Before any deployment is described as live, the project should publish source, ABI, app ID, deployment transaction, admin policy, receipt policy, test results, and audit status.

Read the contribution protocol guide:
[link to contribution guide]
```

## Email 6: How To Help

Subject: How to contribute without hype
Preview: Pick one claim, find the source, mark the status.

```markdown
Hi,

The best community contribution is often simple:

Pick one claim.
Find the source.
Record the date.
Explain the method.
Mark the status.
Note the limitation.

That is how public verification compounds.

Good first tasks:

- check an asset fact,
- review a public link,
- update a stale snapshot,
- identify an unsupported claim,
- suggest clearer wording.

Start with the verification handbook:
[link to verification handbook]
```
